# Plant Research Manuscript Workflow

## Intake and Outline Gate

Create a project record containing article type, English and Chinese working titles, target journal when known, central research question, species, genotype, tissue or cell type, developmental stage, treatment, environment, traits, experimental design, controls, replicates, available data and analyses, figures, tables, protocols, source literature, terminology, missing inputs, and current version.

Produce a detailed outline with section and subsection structure, paragraph jobs, result or evidence source for each claim, figure and table plan, word allocation, missing inputs, and reviewer risks. Wait for explicit approval before drafting a new full manuscript.

## Drafting Order

When evidence permits, draft in this order:

1. Results and Analysis
2. Materials and Methods
3. Introduction
4. Discussion
5. Conclusion
6. Abstract, title, and keywords
7. Legends, references, and declarations

## Section Contracts

### Title

Name the system and principal result without unsupported novelty or causal inflation.

### Abstract

Use `problem -> approach -> principal verified results -> bounded interpretation -> significance`. Include no value or conclusion absent from the main text.

### Keywords

Use specific searchable concepts not already exhausted by the title; preserve accepted gene, pathway, species, and trait naming.

### Introduction

Move from field problem to precise knowledge gap, evidence-based rationale, study question, and bounded objective. Verify every substantive external claim.

### Materials and Methods

Report biological materials, growth conditions, treatments, sampling, controls, replication, assays, instruments, software and versions, thresholds, statistical models, multiple-testing procedures, and data availability sufficiently for reproducibility. Never infer missing details.

### Results and Analysis

Report observations in logical evidence order. Tie every statement to supplied data, analysis, figure, or table. State sample size, replicate type, test, effect direction and magnitude, uncertainty, and adjusted significance when available. Do not discuss mechanisms beyond what the result demonstrates.

### Discussion

Use `principal finding -> comparison with literature -> mechanism or interpretation -> alternative explanation -> limitation -> biological or breeding significance`. Distinguish association, prioritization, necessity, sufficiency, biochemical interaction, physiological function, field validation, and breeding value.

### Conclusion

State only conclusions supported by the study, the principal limitation, and the most defensible next step. Do not repeat the abstract or make universal claims.

## Cross-Checks

Ensure values and directions agree across abstract, main text, figures, tables, supplement, and both languages. Keep gene, protein, allele, genotype, accession, treatment, unit, and statistical notation stable. Any missing or contradictory core evidence is `BLOCK`.
