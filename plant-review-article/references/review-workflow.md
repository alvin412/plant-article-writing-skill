# Plant Review Workflow

## Outline Gate

Before drafting a new full review, provide:

- three to five working titles;
- one-sentence review thesis;
- review type and intended readership;
- scope and explicit exclusions;
- section and subsection argument map;
- paragraph jobs and expected claims;
- evidence types and citation slots;
- proposed figures, tables, and boxes;
- unresolved questions, likely reviewer risks, and word allocation;
- databases, dates, screening logic, and appraisal plan when applicable.

Wait for explicit approval. An already approved user-supplied outline satisfies this gate.

## Argument Architecture

Use:

`field pressure -> plant-specific problem -> precise scope -> organizing principle -> grouped evidence -> disagreements and gaps -> reasoned synthesis -> future experiments and crop or mechanistic value`

Organize by mechanism, biological scale, technology, pathway, evidence class, or translational route. Do not use author chronology as the main structure.

Each paragraph should perform one job:

`synthesis claim -> grouped evidence -> contrast or tension -> interpretation -> evidence boundary -> transition`

## Review Types

- Narrative review: transparent search basis and reasoned evidence selection.
- Perspective: explicit thesis, bounded stance, alternatives, and testable proposals.
- Scoping review: reproducible mapping question, screening, and evidence landscape.
- Systematic review: reproducible search, screening, appraisal, and synthesis.
- Meta-analysis: predefined outcome harmonization, effect-size model, heterogeneity, bias, sensitivity analysis, and software.

Do not label work systematic when its methods cannot be reproduced.

## Evidence Boundaries

- Expression or co-expression supports association or candidate involvement.
- Mutant or transgenic phenotypes support functional contribution; claim necessity or sufficiency only when the design establishes it.
- Binding, phosphorylation, or physical interaction establishes the measured molecular relation, not whole-plant adaptation by itself.
- Cross-species conservation and breeding value require matched evidence.
- Field or multi-environment claims require field or multi-environment validation.

Use `candidate gene` or `strong candidate` until causal evidence supports stronger language. Avoid unsupported `first`, `novel`, `breakthrough`, `master regulator`, `comprehensive`, and universal claims.

## Drafting and Synthesis

Draft after the corpus and claim-evidence map are ready. Write the Introduction and scope, synthesis sections, contradictions and limitations, application boundaries, future directions, figures and tables, then abstract, title, and keywords.

The abstract should move through:

`field problem -> recent progress -> synthesis gap -> scope and organizing principle -> integrated insight -> future direction`

End with specific unresolved mechanisms and decisive experiments, not a generic summary.

## Plant Precision

Name species, genotype, tissue or cell type, developmental stage, treatment, stress, environment, and trait whenever they change interpretation. Preserve stable terminology for genes, proteins, alleles, haplotypes, accessions, pathways, abbreviations, units, and statistics.
