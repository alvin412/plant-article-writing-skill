# Systematic Review and Literature Search

## Contents

1. Review types
2. Search record
3. Screening
4. Appraisal and synthesis
5. Evidence rescue

## 1. Review Types

Choose explicitly:

- narrative review
- perspective
- scoping review
- systematic review
- systematic review with meta-analysis

Do not claim systematic methods unless the search and screening process is reproducible.

## 2. Search Record

Record:

- research or review question
- concepts, synonyms, gene/protein aliases, species names, crop names, traits, stresses, tissues, and methods
- databases and platforms
- exact search strings
- search dates
- year and language restrictions
- article-type filters
- inclusion and exclusion criteria
- update-search date

Use reliable structured sources first. Search multiple sources when coverage matters. Deduplicate by DOI, then normalized title and first author.

## 3. Screening

Maintain counts and reasons:

- identified
- deduplicated
- title/abstract screened
- full text assessed
- included
- excluded with reason
- unavailable full text

For systematic reviews, prepare a PRISMA-style flow record. For scoping reviews, document the mapping purpose and breadth. For narrative reviews, still record the search basis and selection rationale.

## 4. Appraisal and Synthesis

Appraise evidence by:

- study design
- biological replication
- plant-system match
- genetic or biochemical validation
- environmental and developmental context
- statistical adequacy
- independent replication
- field or cross-species validation

Synthesize by mechanism, scale, method, evidence strength, disagreement, or translation route. Do not use paper-by-paper chronology as the main architecture.

For meta-analysis, define outcome harmonization, effect-size model, heterogeneity, publication bias, sensitivity analysis, and software before analysis. Do not fabricate effect sizes from incomplete reports.

## 5. Evidence Rescue

Trigger a new search when:

- a major claim has no source
- only one source supports a broad statement
- recent progress is discussed using only old non-seminal sources
- the cited model, species, tissue, treatment, or method does not match
- contradictory evidence is missing
- a review is used where a primary study is required

After searching:

1. verify metadata
2. read the paper
3. grade support
4. assign it to an exact claim slot
5. update the search record and corpus version

Maintain a verified reference-manager library during the search. Export BibTeX, RIS, ENW, or Zotero-compatible records when required, preserving DOI/PMID and avoiding invented fields.
