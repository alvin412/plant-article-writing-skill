# Citation and Full-Paper Close-Reading Audit

## Contents

1. Source record
2. Full-paper close reading
3. Claim-citation audit
4. Exact source passage
5. Final literature document

## 1. Source Record

For every cited paper record:

- citation key
- full title and authors
- journal, year, volume, issue, pages
- DOI, PMID/PMCID, URL, Zotero key
- article type and publication status
- open-access, user-provided, licensed, or metadata-only access state
- correction, retraction, or expression-of-concern status
- plant species, tissue/cell type, genotype, developmental stage, treatment, stress, and trait
- evidence type and validation depth

## 2. Full-Paper Close Reading

Read all substantive available sections, not only the abstract. Produce bilingual analytical notes rather than reproducing the article.

For each section capture:

```text
Section:
Source anchors:
English analytical note:
Academic Chinese note:
Key evidence:
Figures/tables:
Plant context:
Evidence strength:
Limitations and alternative explanations:
Potential manuscript uses:
```

For Results, follow the evidence chain and note the relevant figures/tables. For Discussion, separate authors' interpretation from directly demonstrated findings.

If full text is unavailable, label the record `partial-reading` and do not imply full-paper verification.

## 3. Claim-Citation Audit

Assign a stable claim ID to each material external claim in the manuscript.

For every citation occurrence record:

```text
Claim ID:
Manuscript sentence:
Manuscript location:
Cited source:
Claim type:
Support grade:
Matched entities and conditions:
Mismatch or scope drift:
Primary or review evidence:
Audit decision:
```

Support grades:

- `strong`: directly tests and supports the relationship in a matching context
- `partial`: supports only part of the sentence or a narrower/different context
- `background`: supports field context but not the specific assertion
- `contradictory`: challenges or limits the sentence
- `metadata-only`: relevance inferred only from metadata
- `not-supported`: does not support the sentence

`metadata-only` and `not-supported` cannot remain as final evidence.

## 4. Exact Source Passage

For each manuscript citation, include the source passage that justifies the citation:

- exact source anchor: page, section, paragraph/block, figure, or table
- short original excerpt
- faithful academic Chinese translation
- explanation of how it supports the manuscript sentence
- support grade and boundary

Use exact quotations conservatively. Do not reproduce a complete copyrighted paper. For copyrighted material not supplied by the user, use short excerpts and full analytical notes. Longer source text is allowed only when the source is user-provided, licensed for that use, or clearly open access.

If no exact supporting passage can be located, mark the citation `BLOCK` until the claim is changed or a suitable source is found.

## 5. Literature Document Requirements

The third Word file must contain:

1. Search and screening summary.
2. Bibliographic integrity summary.
3. Claim-citation matrix.
4. One hierarchical chapter per cited paper.
5. Full-paper bilingual close-reading notes.
6. One entry per use of that source in the manuscript.
7. Original supporting excerpt and Chinese translation.
8. Unresolved evidence gaps and manual checks.

The document must allow bidirectional tracing:

`manuscript claim -> citation -> paper -> source passage`

and:

`paper -> cited manuscript locations -> exact use and support grade`.
