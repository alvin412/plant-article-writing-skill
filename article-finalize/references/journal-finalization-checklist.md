# Target-Journal Finalization Checklist

## 1. Requirement Sources

Prefer the journal's official author instructions, article-type page, reference guide, manuscript template, reporting checklist, and submission-system guidance. Record source URL or supplied file, access date, applicable article type, and any conflict between sources. Use the newest authoritative requirement and disclose unresolved conflicts.

## 2. Manuscript Structure

Check title page, author and affiliation format, corresponding author, abstract type and length, keywords, heading hierarchy, section order, word limits, abbreviations, acknowledgements, references, figures, tables, supplement, and required declarations. Check anonymization and line numbering when required.

## 3. References

Verify title, authors, journal, year, volume, issue, pages or article number, DOI or PMID, publication status, and correction or retraction notices before formatting. Check in-text and bibliography correspondence, ordering, punctuation, author truncation, journal abbreviations, DOI presentation, and treatment of preprints, datasets, software, and websites.

Formatting cannot rescue unsupported citations. Report substantive claim-support problems separately and block submission when material.

## 4. Scientific Nomenclature and Typography

Create an authoritative terminology ledger. Apply journal and organism-specific rules rather than a universal shortcut.

At minimum check:

- species binomials and accepted abbreviations;
- gene and allele symbols, including required italics and capitalization;
- protein names in roman type unless the authoritative rule explicitly differs;
- mutant, genotype, transgene, construct, transcript, locus, and haplotype distinctions;
- Greek letters, superscripts, subscripts, units, statistical symbols, and chemical notation;
- consistent naming across text, tables, figures, legends, and supplement.

Apply italics as Word character formatting, not markdown markers. Do not italicize an entire phrase merely because it contains a gene symbol.

## 5. English Language

Apply the journal's spelling convention, punctuation, capitalization, abbreviations, number style, tense, voice, and terminology. Remove ambiguity, repetition, unsupported promotional language, and claim inflation without altering verified scientific meaning. Keep results observational and discussion interpretive.

## 6. Figures, Tables, and Declarations

Check file format, resolution, dimensions, colour mode, panel labels, fonts, legends, callouts, numbering, units, sample sizes, error bars, tests, significance notation, image integrity, and source data. Verify Data Availability, Code Availability, materials or protocol availability, CRediT contributions, funding, conflicts, ethics, acknowledgements, AI disclosure, and supplementary descriptions when applicable.

## 7. Bilingual Compliance Report

Report in English and academic Chinese:

- manuscript identity and journal requirement sources;
- requirement-by-requirement status;
- reference and nomenclature audit;
- gene and protein typography changes;
- language and structural changes;
- figures, tables, declarations, and file checks;
- unresolved `WARN` and `BLOCK` items;
- rendering and visual-inspection status;
- final submission-readiness decision.
